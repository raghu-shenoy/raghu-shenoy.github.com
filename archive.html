HTML - Hyper Text Markup(Moody???) Language

Are you a designer? Are you an artist? If you are then you are gonna love HTML. But if your sense of art has not evolved beyond the average stone age male(true in my case), you are still gonna love HTML because the kind of stuff you can develop using HTML, tugs at your most basic primal instinct of beauty. My first experience of HTML was  in my preteens. I was like a 12 year kid interested to see how all the colorful websites I saw on the computer worked. But once I got to know that there is so much of stuff to be remembered I just got bored and I moved on. Later on I had to work on HTML since my job demanded it. But it was the most basic of stuff which I could do. It was at this time I heard of something called CSS. I also realized that a website designed for one particular browser may not work as anticipated in another. GIT - I am not abusing, I am distributing.

"I'm an egotistical bastard, and I name all my projects after myself. First Linux, now git."-Linus Torvalds
(Note that Torvalds did not in fact name Linux.)
That's what Linus Torvalds, the developer of Linux and Git had to say when he was asked why did he name the free, fast and efficient Version Control System(VCS), which he developed, as GIT .
The Reason
I guess most of us have worked with VCS at some point or the other. I had previously worked with a Subversion client to collaborate code with my team mates. In the beginning we ate up a lot of each others code(due to bad commits) as all of us where pretty new to the concept of VCS. There were a lot of sparks flying around and a lot of over writes being swept under the carpet. Finally we had a sit down with our boss who then gave us a crash course on  how to "diff" files before a commit, how to check out and so on. Things went on pretty well after that.
